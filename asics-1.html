The Runners’ Strength & Stretch Guide: Glutes and Hip Flexors

Requirements: Barbells or Dumbbells, Straps or Band, Knee Pad, Bench, Machines(Optional)

15
reps (each side)

3
sets
Details: Perform a lateral lunge while crossing one leg behind the other, akin to a curtsy. Use a strap or band to maintain balance and focus the load on the heel.
Note: You only need to step behind your front foot. Crossing far outside your front foot is unnecessary and will lead to uneven hip posture during the exercise.
10
reps (each side)

3
sets
Details: Perform walking lunges with a barbell placed behind your neck. Ensure each knee lightly taps the ground to engage the glutes properly.
Note: May swap of barbell for dumbbells or body weight.
10-15
reps

4
sets
Detail: Squat to a 90-degree angle, then powerfully, but with control in one motion thrust your hips upward as you rise, tightly flexing your glutes.
Note: Aim for 70% of your max repetition weight or above.
15
reps (each side)

3
sets
Details: Perform hip thrusts with one leg, using a bench for support. Keep the thrust controlled, and focus on squeezing the glutes at the top of the movement.
Note: Use a bench.
30
reps (each side)

3
sets
Details: From a tabletop position, lift one leg away from the body, keeping the knee bent. This exercise targets the gluteus medius and minimus for improved lateral stability.
30
secs (each side)

3
sets
Details: From a tabletop position, lift one leg away from the body, keeping the knee bent. This exercise targets the gluteus medius and minimus for improved lateral stability.
Note: Use foam pad or mat to protect knee.
